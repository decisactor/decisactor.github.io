[Medical explanation]

Medical explanation: BMI index (ie body mass index, also known as body mass index, body mass index, English for Body Mass Index, referred to as BMI), is the number of kilograms of body weight divided by the square meter of height, is currently used internationally. A measure of how fat and thin the body is and whether it is healthy. Mainly used for statistical purposes, BMI is a neutral and reliable indicator when we need to compare and analyze the health effects of a person's weight on people of different heights. Body mass index 24-27.9 is overweight; greater than or equal to 28 is obese.

[Common causes and consequences]

BMI is recognized worldwide as a grading method for assessing the degree of obesity. The World Health Organization (WHO) also defines obesity or overweight by BMI. The body mass index is increased, and the incidence of coronary heart disease and stroke will also increase. Overweight and obesity are independent risk factors for coronary heart disease and stroke. For every 2 increase in body mass index, the relative risk of coronary heart disease, stroke, and ischemic stroke increased by 15.4%, 6.1%, and 18.8%, respectively. Once the body mass index reaches or exceeds 24, the probability of serious health-threatening diseases such as hypertension, diabetes, coronary heart disease, and dyslipidemia increases significantly.

[Suggest]

The weight management program can use 4 therapies: diet and nutrition counseling, behavioral therapy, medications and surgery, and regular hospital specialist treatment.

Poor eyesight

[Medical explanation]

At present, the main use of the eye chart is to check the central visual acuity, that is, to check the central visual acuity of the macular area of the retina, so that the initial situation of visual function can be easily and quickly understood, and the clinical diagnosis and treatment of eye diseases are of great significance. The visual acuity is generally divided into two categories: far vision and near vision. The far vision is mostly based on the international standard visual acuity chart. The normal distance vision should be above 1.0. Vision is divided into normal vision, moderate vision impairment, severe visual impairment and blindness. Moderate visual impairment and severe visual impairment and blindness represent a variety of visual impairments.

[Common causes and consequences]

Globally, the main causes of visual impairment include uncorrected refractive errors (myopia, hyperopia, or astigmatism), cataracts without surgery, and glaucoma. Other factors affecting vision, in addition to the disease of the eye itself (such as keratitis), systemic diseases (such as diabetes), drug effects, and eye hygiene, eating habits, psychological factors, environmental factors and accidental injuries, etc., may be Short-term or long-term effects on vision. In addition, the computer as one of our more eye-catching tools is one of the main causes of eye fatigue, namely computer vision syndrome, which mainly manifests as dry eyes, decreased vision, and even dizziness. Blindness and visual impairment seriously affect the physical health and quality of life of the people, and increase the burden on families and society. This is a major public health problem.

[Suggest]

1 slow down the eye fatigue caused by excessive use of computer; 2 ensure sufficient outdoor activity time; 3 often use more eyes, especially those with more computers, should eat more foods containing vitamin A, such as animal liver, green leafy vegetables, carrots Wait. 4 lower than normal vision is recommended to go to regular hospital specialist treatment, including medical optometry, wearing appropriate glasses and so on.

[Medical explanation]

Serum total cholesterol (TC) refers to the sum of cholesterol contained in various lipoproteins, derived from food and liver synthesis. Cholesterol is an important raw material for the production of physiologically active substances such as adrenocortical hormone, bile acid and vitamin D, and is also a major component of the cell membrane. The concentration of total cholesterol in serum can be used as an indicator to determine whether lipid metabolism is normal.

[Common causes and consequences]

The main function of low-density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL-C) is to transport cholesterol to extrahepatic tissue cells to meet their cholesterol needs.
